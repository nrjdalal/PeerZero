PeerZero is a **local-only BitTorrent client** in a single web app. Paste a magnet
link (or add a `.torrent`), and watch it download with live progress - all running on
your own machine. Nothing is hosted; there's no account and no cloud, and no data
leaves your computer except the torrent traffic itself.

> **For educational purposes only.** PeerZero is a personal tool for learning about
> BitTorrent, peer-to-peer networking, and web development. Only download content you
> are legally entitled to - Linux distributions, public-domain works, Creative Commons
> media, and files you own or have permission to share. You are responsible for
> complying with copyright law in your jurisdiction.

## How it's laid out

The app centers on the **Transfers** tab: everything you've added, with live progress,
speed, peers, ETA, and controls to pause, resume, or remove - all streaming over one
live connection to the download engine. See
[Downloading & transfers](/docs/downloading).

## Quick start

1. Start the app with `bun run dev` and open the printed URL.
2. Paste a **magnet link** into the box (for example, a Linux distribution's official
   magnet) and add it.
3. Watch it download on the **Transfers** tab. It auto-stops when finished - PeerZero
   is a downloader and doesn't seed.

## Good to know

- **Seeds matter most.** A torrent with 0 seeders can't download - there's no one to
  download from.
- **Downloads finishing then stopping is expected** - PeerZero pauses a torrent once
  it completes instead of seeding it.
- **Slow downloads or a flaky connection are almost always your network**, not the
  app - most often too many connections overwhelming your router. See
  [Troubleshooting](/docs/troubleshooting).
